Luke Snow

I am a 4th-year PhD candidate in the Electrical & Computer Engineering Department at Cornell University. I'm advised by Prof. Vikram Krishnamurthy, and supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. In 2021 I graduated summa cum laude from Clemson University with a B.S. in electrical engineering and a minor in mathematics, and received the W.M. Riggs Award for the most outstanding senior in electrical engineering.

I have professional experience in radar and intelligent systems analysis through internships with the U.S. Army Research Laboratory (2022), MIT Lincoln Laboratory (2021), and Leidos Dynetics (2019).

I am a member of the Cornell Statistical Signal Processing Lab, and am affiliated with the Foundations of Informations, Networks and Decision Systems (FIND) research collective.

Research

Broadly, my interests are in statistical signal processing, inverse reinforcement learning and game theory. My work has taken application in the following fields:
Social Sensing : we address the interface between human decision making and signal processing, sensing and control strategies
Multi-Agent Intelligent Systems: we aim to discover and control the underlying strategic mechanisms governing observed multi-agent group behavior
Adaptive Inverse Learning : we develop and analyze Langevin dynamics algorithms for real-time inverse reinforcement learning.
