Luke Snow

I am a 4th-year PhD candidate in the Electrical & Computer Engineering Department at Cornell University, where I am supported by a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. I am advised by Prof. Vikram Krishnamurthy, and am a member of the Cornell Statistical Signal Processing Lab. Before Cornell, I graduated from Clemson University with a B.S. in electrical engineering and a minor in mathematics.

Research

Broadly, my interests are in statistical signal processing, inverse reinforcement learning and game theory.

Social Sensing: addressing the interface between human decision making and signal processing, sensing and control strategies.

Multi-Agent Intelligent Systems: discovering and controlling the underlying strategic mechanisms governing observed multi-agent group behavior
